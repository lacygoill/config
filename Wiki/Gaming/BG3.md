# Race

Each  race  comes  with  "race  features".   When  you  select  a  feature  with
hyperlinks, you  can click  on them by  pressing the touch  pad button  (this is
repeatable; i.e. if a newly-open tooltip has hyperlinks too, you can again press
the touch pad button).

Also, each race is proficient with certain  weapons and armours.  When you use a
weapons you're  proficient with, your proficiency  bonus will be added  when you
roll a dice for an attack.

When you use  an armour you're *not* proficient with,  your're at a disadvantage
when you attack.

After you have selected a race and a subrace, you can customize your character's
appearance.

# "Advantage"

It makes you more likely to succeed a  roll dice; 2 rolls are performed, and the
higher value is used.

# "Disadvantage"

It makes you less likely to succeed a  roll dice; 2 rolls are performed, and the
lower value is used.

# cantrips

You can use these unlimited; in each battle, and even multiple times in the same
battle.

# spells

You can only use these a limited number of times.
After that, you need a long rest to restore them.

# background

Your background adds some skills to your character.

Stealth is helpful: it allows you to get a surprise attack on your enemies.

# finesse weapon

A weapon that scales off of dexterity instead of strength.

# stats

Max out your main stat (e.g. charisma for bard).

Constitution is nice to have (HP max, stamina, endurance).

Strength:

   - effectiveness with melee weapons
   - how much you can carry
   - how far you can jump (useful  to access secrets paths or backroutes  that
     lets you sneak up on enemies)

Dexterity affects:

   - your effectiveness with ranged and finesse weapons
   - your initiative
   - your armour class

Intelligence improves spellcasting for wizards.

Charisma improves spellcasting for bards, paladins, sorcerers, and warlocks.
Also influences traders' prices.
Charismatic people are more likely to win people over.

---

Suppose a  roll is done  to check your  charisma, and you  roll a 13  while your
charisma is  17.  The result  will be  `13 + 3 = 16`, because every  even number
beyond 10 is an extra point to your dice roll.

Similarly, if  one of  your abilities  is below 10  (e.g. strength),  every even
number below 10 is subtracted for a roll check of that type.

# Actions

You get one action, and one bonus action (a spell, a cantrip, ...) per turn.

# "Saving Throw"

It determines  whether a target  resists a spell or  condition (i.e. you  have a
chance to resist something bad by throwing a dice).

# "High Ground"

When this bonus is displayed while hovering the cursor on an enemy, your're less
likely to miss a ranged attack.

# "Jump"

Jump is useful to jump over/onto something.

It's also  useful to  jump out  of a  slippery substance,  and prevent  you from
falling over and going prone (i.e. you miss a turn).

The higher you are, the further you can jump.

# "Hide"

Hide allows you to become stealth (enemies won't see you).
It also allows you to sneak up on  enemies (to do extra damage), and to surprise
them (they miss an entire turn).

When you hide, a sun icon appears  on your cursor.  It indicates how visible you
are.  You  want it to be  empty, or at  least half-empty.  When it's  full, it's
harder to pass a stealth check.

You can  get into  dark spaces  by shooting out  the lights  or turning  off the
torches in a cave, which increases your chances of passing a stealth check.

A red area represents the line of sight of the enemies nearby.

To hide and move a single character, toggle group mode.

# "Throw"

You can throw  a character, or an  item from the world /  your inventory (useful
for example to throw a potion to an ally and heal them immediately).

If you throw a  weapon at an enemy, it will do that  weapon's damage, unless the
weapon is missing the  "thrown" tag (in which case it will  probably just do one
damage).

The higher you are, the more impact damage an object thrown at an enemy will do.

# "Dash"

Dash can be used to double the distance you can travel (useful to disengage from
a losing battle).

# "Flee Combat"

You have to  get far enough to flee  from combat.  When you do,  a "flee combat"
button lights  up, and you  can click on  it to flee.  You  have to do  that for
every of your characters.

# "Disengage"

If an  enemy has a red  arrow pointing at you,  it means that if  you run passed
them or away  from them, they have  an "opportunity attack": it's  a free attack
that they get out of their turn.

This  is when  the "Disengage"  action  is useful;  it prevents  the enemy  from
getting an "opportunity attack".

# "Dip"

The "Dip" bonus action lets you dip your weapon into some element (e.g. fire) to
do more damage.  You can find fire on  the ground if an enemy shot a fire arrow,
or by setting a candle.

Same thing with  poison; you can throw  a poison bottle on the  ground, then dip
your weapon into it.

# "Shove"

It's a bonus action that  lets you push an enemy off of high  ledges to do a lot
of damage (possibly kill them).  Warning: If you push them into a place that you
can't go, you won't be able to loot their corpse.

It's also useful to  push an ally out of an area of  effect damage ability (e.g.
fire), or to wake them up if they've been put to sleep.

# "Improvised melee weapon"

Sometimes  your  weapon is  not  effective  against  an  enemy, or  you've  been
disarmed.  In  that case,  you can  pick up  an object  nearby and  use it  as a
weapon.

# "Help"

When a character goes down, they're not dead, they're just laying down.  They're
going through some dice rolls: each time  it's their turn, they're going to roll
their dice.  Either they  pass or fail.  If they succeed  3 times, they'll stand
up on their own accord.  If they fail 3 times, they'll die.

"Help" lets  you help a down  character stand back  up again.  It also  lets you
remove  Burning,  Ensnared,  Entangled,  Enwebbed, Prone  and  Sleeping  from  a
character.

Alternatively, you  can throw a potion  at a down  character, or cast a  heal on
them; useful, if you're not in range to "Help".

# spell slot

You can use a  high-level spell slot to cast a low-level spell  and make it more
effective.

##
# How to make the camera automatically follow my character?

    Options
    > select "Gameplay settings" tab
    > set "auto-rotate camera" to "on"

# How to pull the camera away from the character (to see more of your surroundings)?

Move the right stick down once or multiple times.

---

To get back down into your regular camera view, press the right stick up once or
multiple times.

# How to scout ahead?

Press on the left stick.
It will give you a cursor that you can move around.
To get back to your character, just press the left stick again.

# How to examine something (corpse, enemy, fire on the ground, ...)?

Hover  the cursor  over  it (scout  ahead  if necessary),  then  press the  down
directional button.  This works too if you're targeting an enemy or a character.

# How to select a different character?

Press L2, select the desired character, and press cross.

# How to separate a character out of the rest of the group?

Press L2, select the desired character, and press triangle.
To join the whole group back together, press L2, then press square long enough.

# How to create sub-groups?

Press L2, select  the desired character, and press triangle.   Do that for every
character you want in your sub-group.   Repeat the process, but this time, press
square to join the splitted characters back into a sub-group.

# How to enter stealth mode?

Press the down directional button long enough.
Press it again, to quit stealth mode.

# How to see which weapons/armours I'm proficient with?

Press R2, move the left stick up to select "Character Sheet", press cross, press
R1 three times  to select the "Proficiencies" tab: below  "skills", you can find
the  "simple  weapons",  "martial  weapons", and  "armour  and  shields"  you're
proficient with.

# How to turn off automatic radial menus?

    Options
    > Interface
    > Hotbar Options
    > disable every entry in this section

# How to manually set a radial slot?

Press L1 or R1 to display the radials, then select the desired slot with L3, and
press square.  A menu should open with the following entries:

   - Set Slot
   - Swap Slot
   - Clear Slot
   - Add Radial
   - Remove Radial

##
# How to close a game?

Press the  PS button to  bring your bottom menu  up, then select  the "switcher"
button, select your game, and finally press "Close Game".

Alternatively, perform  a long press  of the PS  button, then press  the options
button, and press "Close Game".
